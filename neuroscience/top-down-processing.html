Top-Down Processing

Definition

Top-down processing involves perceiving things based on your prior experiences and knowledge. It refers to how our brain utilizes existing knowledge and expectations to interpret what we see, hear, feel, touch or even taste. It's a shortcut the brain uses in moments where it has to interpret information that might be new, ambiguous or limited, and to make decisions quickly by using prior, familiar experiences as a guide.
