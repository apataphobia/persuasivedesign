IKEA Effect

Definition

The IKEA effect is the phenomenon where people place a disproportionately high value on products they partially created themselves.

Psychological Impact

Investment of effort leads users to appreciate and value outcomes more highly, even if objectively they aren't superior.

Design Application

Allow customization, configuration, or user-driven creation to increase emotional ownership and product satisfaction.
