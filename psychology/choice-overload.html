Choice Overload

Definition

Choice overload refers to the cognitive burden placed on consumers when faced with too many options, often leading to decision fatigue or avoidance.

Psychological Impact

When presented with many options, people are less likely to make a decision at all or feel less satisfied with their choice due to anticipated regret.

Design Application

Limit available options or use smart defaults to simplify choices and encourage action. Bundle similar options when appropriate.
